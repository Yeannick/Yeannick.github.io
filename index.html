GOAP research

As part of my coursework in gameplay AI programming, I conducted research on Goal-Oriented Action Planning (GOAP) as a behavior system. GOAP utilizes A* pathfinding to navigate a list of actions, each with their own set of conditions and effects, in order to determine a sequence of actions that the AI needs to execute.

For the execution phase, I implemented a Finite State Machine (FSM) to handle the planning of actions, movement towards Points of Interest (POI) for actions, and the actual execution of actions. This approach was aimed at minimizing the number of actions that the pathfinding algorithm had to traverse.

Graduation work BVH-Acceleration for raytracing

For my graduation work, I conducted research on BVH (Bounding Volume Hierarchy) acceleration techniques for real-time ray tracing rendering. I focused on testing various types of volumes and building algorithms to optimize the performance of the rendering process.

I was able to significantly optimize the raytracing renderer we developed in the Graphics Programming course. Initially, it achieved around 1 frame per second (fps) for a single model. However, through optimizations and improvements, I managed to boost the performance to approximately 30 fps when rendering 1024 instances of the same model.

Deferred renderer DirectX 11

As part of the Graphics Programming course, we developed a rasterized forward renderer using DirectX 11. Building upon this foundation, I extended the project by implementing deferred rendering.
